Ch. 1 — Electrons, Bonds & Molecular Properties
Lewis structures · VSEPR · hybridization · polarity · IMF
Master Kit: Essential
0%
▾
Study Goal
Turn Honors Chem tools into organic-ready instincts. Achieve automatic fluency with Lewis structures, formal charge, hybridization, and polarity.

HW Syllabus Topics
Electronic structure · Lewis structures · periodic trends · VSEPR · polarity · intermolecular forces
Model Kit Usage
Build sp³ (tetrahedral), sp² (trigonal planar), sp (linear) carbons and compare bond angles directly.
Start with: methane → ethylene → acetylene.

Online Resources
  • KhanStructure & Bonding overview
  • KhanLewis diagrams (practice)
  • KhanVSEPR theory
  • MolView3D structure viewer
Study Checklist
  • Read Klein Ch.1; mark δ+ and δ− on 10 bonds
  • Solve SkillBuilders; target 80–90% accuracy on formal charge
  • Build sp³/sp²/sp carbons with model kit; compare bond angles
  • Complete one-page Ch.1 rule sheet + missed-problem list
Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Omitting lone pairs on O and N atoms
  • Formal charge calculation errors (bonds vs electrons)
  • Wrong hybridization assignment
  • Reversing δ+/δ− polarity direction
Self-check
Can I mark δ+/δ− on C=O, C−Cl, O−H, N−H without hesitation?

Master Kit: Essential
0%
▾
Study Goal
Become fully fluent in bond-line structures. Convert between condensed, Lewis, and skeletal forms; count hidden hydrogens without effort.

HW Syllabus Topics
Molecular representations · bond-line structures · functional groups · 3D visualization
Model Kit Usage
Draw a 2D structure first, then build it with the kit and compare. Practice wedge/dash notation against the physical model.

Online Resources
  • KhanBond-line structures
  • Khan3D bond-line structures
  • MolView2D → 3D conversion
Study Checklist
  • Practice condensed ↔ bond-line conversions (20 structures)
  • Label heteroatoms, lone pairs, and charges on each structure
  • Recognize 10–15 common functional groups by sight
  • Build 5 structures with kit; verify hidden H count
Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Forgetting that vertices and endpoints represent carbon
  • Missing hydrogens on carbon atoms
  • Omitting lone pairs and H on heteroatoms (O, N)
Self-check
Can I read an unfamiliar bond-line structure and count carbons and hydrogens without stopping?

Ch. 3 — Acids & Bases
pKa · conjugate pairs · equilibrium direction · stability factors
Master Kit: Recommended
0%
▾
Study Goal
Use pKa and conjugate base stability to predict acid/base direction and justify with resonance, induction, and atom size.

HW Syllabus Topics
Acid/base foundations · mechanisms · electronic structure · functional-group reactivity
Model Kit Usage
Use kit to visualize resonance structures in 3D — especially for delocalized systems (acetate, amide). Helps track electron movement without moving atoms.

Online Resources
  • KhanOrganic acid-base mechanisms
  • KhanBrønsted-Lowry acids & bases
  • KhanResonance & formal charge (practice)
Study Checklist
  • Create a pKa mini-table for common organic functional groups
  • For 10 reactions: identify acid, base, conjugates, and favored side
  • Draw resonance structures for acetate, allyl cation, amide
  • Build acid/base decision checklist with stability factors
Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Reading pKa backwards (lower pKa = stronger acid)
  • Choosing the strongest species as the product
  • Ignoring resonance/induction/atom size in stability
Self-check
Can I choose the more acidic proton and explain why using conjugate base stability?

Ch. 4 — Alkanes & Cycloalkanes
Nomenclature · Newman projections · chair conformations · IMF
Strong familiarity Kit: Essential
0%
▾
Study Goal
Learn naming and conformation basics. Understand Newman projections and cyclohexane chair flips — very difficult without the model kit.

HW Syllabus Topics
Nomenclature · 3D shapes · IMF / physical properties · conformational analysis
Model Kit Usage ★ Most Used
Build ethane and rotate front/back carbons to see staggered vs eclipsed.
Build cyclohexane and physically perform the chair flip — axial/equatorial swap becomes instantly clear.
Cannot be understood from 2D drawings alone.

Online Resources
  • Leah4SciNewman projections (video series)
  • ChemStepsChair → Newman conversion
  • KhanAlkane structures & naming
  • MolViewView cyclohexane in 3D
Study Checklist
  • Name 15 simple alkanes and cycloalkanes correctly
  • Build ethane with kit; draw staggered and eclipsed Newman projections
  • Build cyclohexane; perform chair flip and identify axial/equatorial positions
  • Complete naming checklist + Newman projection examples
Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Choosing wrong parent chain in nomenclature
  • Numbering errors (start from the end giving lowest locants)
  • Confusing front and back carbons in Newman projections
  • Flipping axial/equatorial labels incorrectly after chair flip
Self-check
Can I draw a Newman projection from a 3D structure and identify the most stable conformation?

Ch. 5 — Stereoisomerism
Chirality · R/S configuration · enantiomers · diastereomers · Fischer projections
Strong familiarity Kit: Most Critical
0%
▾
Study Goal
Understand chirality and all stereoisomer relationships. Find chiral centers; assign R/S; distinguish enantiomers from diastereomers.

HW Syllabus Topics
Stereochemistry · 3D visualization · chirality · enantiomers · diastereomers · R/S
Model Kit Usage ★ Most Critical Chapter
Build two mirror-image molecules and physically try to superimpose them.
Assign R/S by holding the model with priority 4 pointing away — exactly as the rule requires.
Model kit is strongly recommended by the syllabus for this chapter.

Online Resources
  • KhanStereochemistry series
  • Leah4SciKit + chirality walkthrough
  • App (iOS)Visualizing Organic Chemistry
  • MolView3D enantiomer comparison
Study Checklist
  • Identify all chiral centers in 10 different molecules
  • Build two enantiomers with kit; verify non-superimposability
  • Assign R/S to 15 chiral centers using priority rules
  • Classify pairs as enantiomers, diastereomers, or identical
Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Calling non-chiral carbons chiral (check: 4 different groups required)
  • Forgetting to put priority 4 pointing away before assigning R/S
  • Confusing enantiomers and diastereomers
  • Assuming mirror image = enantiomer (must check non-superimposability)
Self-check
Can I build two mirror-image molecules and explain whether they are enantiomers or identical?

Ch. 6 — Chemical Reactivity & Mechanisms
Curved arrows · nucleophile / electrophile · leaving groups · intermediates
Familiarity Kit: Recommended
0%
▾
Study Goal
Learn the curved-arrow language and identify reactivity roles (nucleophile, electrophile, leaving group). Foundation for all future mechanisms.

HW Syllabus Topics
Reaction mechanisms · functional group transformations · synthesis foundation
Model Kit Usage
Visualize the SN2 backside attack geometry — nucleophile approaching 180° from leaving group. Helps make curved arrow direction intuitive.

Online Resources
  • KhanOrganic acid-base mechanisms
  • Leah4SciCurved arrow rules
  • MolView3D reactant visualization
Study Checklist
  • Learn curved arrow rules: always start from electrons, not atoms
  • Identify Nu, E, and LG in 10 reaction setups
  • Check formal charges after every arrow-pushing step
  • Build mechanism roles checklist (Nu/E/LG/intermediate definitions)
Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Starting curved arrows from an atom (must start from lone pair or bond)
  • Drawing arrows into the wrong site (must go to electron-poor atom)
  • Not checking formal charges after each mechanism step
Self-check
Can I draw a 2-step mechanism with correct arrows and verify all formal charges after each step?

  • Identify cis/trans isomers of simple alkenes

Spectroscopy · IR · NMR · mass spectrometry
Model Kit Usage
Not needed. Use MolView instead — it automatically generates IR spectra and mass spectra for any molecule you draw, which is far more useful for this chapter.

Online Resources
